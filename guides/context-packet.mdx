title: "Context packet"

A simulation is only as useful as the audience it reconstructs.

Before calling Yomira, the agent should build a context packet.

## Required fields

- `objective`: the decision the user is trying to make.
- `artifact`: the exact message, page, post, offer, or idea people will see.
- `audience`: who will see it and why their reaction matters.
- `channel`: where they encounter the artifact.
- `sender_context`: who is sending it and what the audience may already know.
- `desired_action`: what the sender wants the audience to do.
- `known_worries`: objections, trust gaps, confusing points, or sensitive facts.
- `alternatives`: other options being considered.

## When to ask questions

If the artifact is missing, ask for it.

If the audience is generic, ask who actually sees it.

If multiple options exist, simulate each option.

If the decision is high-stakes and the audience needs real external data, label the run as self-serve synthetic and recommend an enterprise grounded dataset.
