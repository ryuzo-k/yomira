The problem

Most AI tools give you a plausible answer. That is not enough when the real question is how different people will interpret what you are about to put into the world. A message can sound clear to you and still feel vague, suspicious, exciting, cheap, credible, or irrelevant to the people who actually see it.

The product

Yomira runs audience simulations and returns the distribution of reactions: who becomes interested, who stays skeptical, who misunderstands the offer, who dismisses it, and what they would actually say in their own words. The output is designed to be used inside Claude Code, Codex, Hermes Agent, Cursor, OpenClaw, scripts, dashboards, and your own product.

Use cases

  • Content preflight — check how a post, article, ad, or GEO page may be received before it goes live.
  • Message decisions — compare sales DMs, follow-ups, investor notes, user interviews, and sensitive outreach before sending.
  • Product and offer positioning — test landing pages, pricing pages, feature explanations, and launch copy against likely buyer reactions.
  • Venture idea checks — see which parts of a new business idea create desire, confusion, distrust, or concrete willingness to try.
  • Embedded simulation products — add reaction simulation to your own workflow, research product, agency tooling, or decision system through the API.

How it works

  1. 1. Send the artifact

    Pass the message, page, content, offer, plan, or product idea you want to test.

  2. 2. Define the audience

    Describe the people whose reactions matter, from broad public audiences to narrow buyer groups.

  3. 3. Run the simulation

    The API creates agent-level reactions in parallel, then aggregates them into distributions and raw voices.

  4. 4. Export the evidence

    Download JSON or Markdown and keep using the result in your agent conversation, report, or product.

API quickstart

curl -s -X POST "https://yomira-api.vercel.app/api/simulate" \
  -H "content-type: application/json" \
  -H "x-api-key: $YOMIRA_API_KEY" \
  -d '{
    "objective": "Should we publish this landing page?",
    "artifact": { "type": "landing_page", "content": "..." },
    "audience": { "description": "founders, marketers, agencies, and AI-agent users" },
    "simulation": { "target_n": 100, "mode": "standard" }
  }'

FAQ

Is this a survey replacement?

Not for every case. It is a fast reaction preflight layer. Grounded enterprise simulations can use more specific external or private data.

Can I use it from an AI agent?

Yes. Give your agent the docs, an API key, and the artifact you want to test. The API returns status, JSON, Markdown, distributions, and voices.

Is the result a single score?

No. The useful part is the distribution of reactions and the raw private voices behind that distribution.

Who should try it first?

People who publish, send, sell, launch, or position things often enough that one misunderstood artifact has a real cost.
